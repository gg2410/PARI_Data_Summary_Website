PARI Data Summary

8.07.2022

Summary Insights

We had 292 Applicants varying among 6 Age groups

As clearly indicated, almost half of our applicants were youngsters. Followed by the age groups 25-34, 45-54, 35-44, 65+ and finally 55-64.


The majority of our responses were submitted by females, followed by males and then some interviewees who preferred not to answer.


Now lets look at the first question about whether our interviewees read about happenings in India or not.

An overwhelming majority of our responses said yes indicating that either we picked a very informed set of interviewees or people may overestimate their response. Yes was followed by 'As often as I can', and No as seen in the graph above.


Do people prefer reading to watching journalism?

No was the most common answer here, followed by yes and both equally with a small gap.


How often do our interviewees seek out new content?

We see our exceptional interviewees once again, with the majority looking for content everyday, followed by less number of people seeking content as the frequency gets lesser. The descent is clearly visible.


Consumption Patterns of the Respondents

The average time spent per session was less than 30 minutes, with similar number of interviewees on both sides of the average.


This chart shows the frequency of people's favorite journalism brands. The Hindu leads the pack, but other mainstream companies like Times of India, and Indian Express are not far behind.


We have a good idea about the interviewees' behavior and demographics now. How did they respond to the questions about PARI?

Responses to Basic Questions about awareness of PARI.


  • Out of 292 responses, 151 interviewees responded no to being aware of PARI, 137 said yes, while 4 responded maybe. Therefore, we had a good mix of people who were interviewed, and the information received takes in a fair bit of input from readers as well as non-readers of PARI.
  • Of the few responses on how people came about PARI, 40 heard about PARI through friends, or word of mouth. This was followed by 28 peopl via various social media platforms, and around 18 through this internship. There are also a few people who knew PARI from past research, or familiarity with employees.
  • The last question regarding PARI was whether people have read any of their work. As expected, the majority responded No at 178. 102 replied affirmatively, while the remaining preferred not to answer.

Responses to Questions About Archives

  • When asked whether they have used archives before, 101 people said yes, 163 said no, and the rest abstained from answering.
  • Coming to popular arcives, JSTOR outweighed all others in frequency. Taylor and Francis was the second most common and the other archives were industry specific in very few numbers.

Satisfaction with Knowledge of India

The most common response was no (187) indicating the need for more India concerning content. This was followed by 80 people saying yes, and the rest were ambivalent towards the question.


This final pie chart depicts the distribution of how people responded to whether they liked finding content or recieving it. 152 applicants chose own content, while 78 said they preferred receiving it. Others either did not respond, or chose to consume content either way.
